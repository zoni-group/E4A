10.1 Start Here

Speaking is easier when you prepare. A short script helps you organize your English before you talk to a teacher, employer, classmate, or community member.

Today you will write a 60-second script, practice pronunciation and timing, and review voice consent rules.

10.2 Why This Matters

AI can help revise a script or suggest clearer words. But your voice, your meaning, and your consent matter. You should not clone, copy, or imitate another real person’s voice. You should not record someone without permission.

Clear speaking plus safe voice habits help you communicate respectfully.

10.4 Core Idea

A script is the words you prepare before you speak. A short script helps you speak clearly and stay calm.

Pronunciation means how words sound. You can practice final sounds, word stress, pausing, and polite tone.

Voice consent means permission to record or use a voice. Use your own voice only. Do not clone, copy, or imitate another real person’s voice.

10.5 Key Vocabulary

Word Simple Meaning Example Sentence
script Written words prepared before speaking I wrote a script for my call.
pronunciation How words sound I practiced pronunciation.
timing How long something takes My script timing is 58 seconds.
pause A short stop while speaking I pause after each step.
consent Permission I need consent to record a voice.
clone Make a copy Do not clone a real person’s voice.
imitate Copy the sound of someone Do not imitate another person’s voice.
feedback Advice to improve My partner gave feedback.
