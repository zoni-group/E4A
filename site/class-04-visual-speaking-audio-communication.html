9.1 Start Here

You see a small flyer on a wall at school:

Meeting Thursday Room 3

You ask yourself: Which Thursday? What time? What is the meeting about? Should I bring something?

Now look at a clearer version:

English Conversation Club
Thursday, June 5
4:00 to 5:00 PM
Library, Room 3
Free and open to all students.
Please bring one question to share.

Now you know if it is for you. The same idea — a small flyer — but the second version uses clear English words and clear visual details. People can read it, understand it, and decide quickly.

Speaking is the same. A short, clear message helps people listen and respond.

9.2 Why This Matters

You will share messages, images, and short spoken English in your daily life — at school, at work, with family, and in your community. Clear visuals and clear speaking help people understand and act. They also help you feel more confident in English. Today you will practice both, and learn safe rules for using images and voice.

9.3 Today You Will

By the end of this lesson, you will:

  1. Describe a visual in simple English.
  2. Learn safe image and voice rules: open licenses, consent, and no private faces or voices.
  3. Write a 60-second English speaking script with a greeting, purpose, request, and thank-you.
  4. Read your script aloud and give a partner kind, helpful feedback.

9.4 Beginner Explanation

A visual is a picture, flyer, or simple diagram. A clear visual helps people understand your message quickly. A script is the words you prepare before you speak. A short script helps you speak more clearly and stay calm.

AI can help you check or improve a visual description or a speaking script. You still need to check that the message is true, safe, respectful, and uses only images and voices you have permission to use.

9.5 Key Vocabulary

Word Simple Meaning Example Sentence

9.8 Guided Practice

9.8.1 Warm-Up: Clear or Confusing Communication?

Look at a short message and a simple image or flyer.

Answer:

What is clear?
What is confusing?
What is missing?

Speaking frames:

The message is clear because __________.
The image is confusing because __________.
One missing detail is __________.

9.8.2 Mini-Lesson: Safe Visuals, Open Licenses, and Consent

Students learn:

  • Do not copy random images without permission.
  • Do not use another person’s face or voice without permission.
  • Do not create misleading images.
  • Use open-license media when possible.
  • Course images provided in this book are ZONI® assets unless otherwise noted.
  • Save images in open formats like .svg or .png.
  • Record only your own voice if recording is allowed.
  • Do not imitate or clone another real person’s voice.

In simple English: first check that the visual is safe to use, then describe it and practice your script.

NoteAttribution Note

For open-license images, give credit using TASL: Title, Author, Source, License. Also name changes you made, such as cropped, resized, translated, or edited (Creative Commons n.d.).

9.8.4 Guided Practice B: Improve a Speaking Script

Original:

I need job. Help me.

Improved:

Hello, my name is Carlos. I am interested in the cashier position.
I have experience helping customers and handling payments.
Could you please tell me how to apply?
Thank you for your time.

Identify:

  1. greeting
  2. purpose
  3. details
  4. request
  5. closing
