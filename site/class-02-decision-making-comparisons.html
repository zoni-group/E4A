5.1 Start Here

A friend asks you a simple question:

What is the best phone plan?

Think about it. There is no single best plan for everyone.

  • A student who calls family abroad needs international minutes.
  • A delivery driver needs a lot of mobile data on the road.
  • A parent at home might prefer a family plan with shared data.

The “best” choice changes for each person. Before you choose anything — a phone plan, a job, a class, a bus or train — you need to know what matters most to you.

5.2 Why This Matters

You make decisions every day. Which job to apply for. Which class to take. How to spend your time and your money. AI can help you see your options and compare them in English. But AI does not know your life. You do. Today you will learn a simple tool to compare choices in clear English — and remember that the final decision is yours.

5.3 Today You Will

By the end of this lesson, you will:

  1. Learn how to build a simple decision matrix in English.
  2. Compare two real-life options using clear comparison phrases.
  3. Practice English like “A is cheaper than B” and “I might choose A because…”
  4. Decide why the final decision must stay with a person, not the AI.

5.4 Beginner Explanation

A decision matrix is a simple table for comparing choices. You pick the things that matter to you (criteria), give each option a score, and write short reasons in English. AI can help you find criteria you forgot or risks you did not see. AI should not choose for you — you make the final decision.

NoteMethod Note

This activity adapts the decision matrix, also called a Pugh matrix, decision grid, or selection matrix. A decision matrix helps compare options against criteria and can use weights when some criteria matter more (American Society for Quality n.d.).

ImportantHuman Decision Rule

AI can help you compare. You make the final decision.

In simple English: AI can give ideas, but you check the facts and make the final choice.

5.5 Key Vocabulary

Word Simple Meaning Example Sentence
option A choice Bus and train are two options.
compare Look at two or more things I compare the cost and time.
criteria The things that matter My criteria are cost and safety.
risk A possible problem The risk is arriving late.
evidence Information that supports an answer The price is evidence.
decision A choice after thinking My decision is to take the bus.

5.6 Sentence Frames

For me, the most important criterion is __________.
Option A is cheaper than Option B.
Option B is more convenient than Option A.
I might choose __________ because __________.
Before I decide, I need to check __________.
AI helped me compare, but I chose __________.

5.8 Guided Practice

5.8.1 Warm-Up: What Does “Best” Mean?

Question:

What is the best phone plan?

Possible criteria:

  • cost
  • data
  • coverage
  • contract
  • family plan
  • international calls

Speaking frame:

For me, the most important criterion is __________ because __________.

5.8.2 Mini-Lesson: Decision Matrix

Basic structure:

Decision:
[What am I choosing?]

Options:
A.
B.
C.

Criteria:
1. Cost
2. Time
3. Convenience
4. Quality
5. Risk

Scoring:

1 = poor
2 = okay
3 = good
4 = very good
5 = excellent

5.8.3 Compare Two Commute Options

Criteria Importance 1-5 Bus Train Notes
Cost 5 5 3 Bus is cheaper.
Time 4 2 5 Train is faster.
Convenience 3 3 4 Train has fewer stops.
Risk 4 3 4 Bus may be late.

Write three comparison sentences:

The bus is cheaper than the train.
The train is faster than the bus.
I might choose the train because time is important.
