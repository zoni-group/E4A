11.1 Start Here

You are close to the final lesson. Open your Student Workbook and look at your work. Each page shows English you wrote and improved.

Today you will check your portfolio, choose a final project task, draft a presentation, and practice aloud.

11.2 Why This Matters

A portfolio is useful only when it is organized and easy to explain. Your presentation helps you show what you learned: asking, checking, comparing, deciding, and explaining in English with safe AI habits.

TipWrite in the Book

Some exercises in the website version are interactive. You can type your answers directly on the page, and you can copy or download a backup if your teacher asks.

The main task is to write in the book, not to create separate files.

11.3 Today You Will

By the end of this lesson, you will:

  1. Use a portfolio checklist for Lessons 1-8.
  2. Choose a safe final project task.
  3. Draft a short English presentation.
  4. Practice aloud with a partner.
  5. Complete the matching Student Workbook page.
NoteLesson Reference

Theme: Students prepare the final portfolio and presentation.

AI Focus: Portfolio review, final task choice, and safe workflow planning.

ESL Focus: Presentation language, process words, reflection, and pronunciation practice.

Content Objective: Students will prepare a final portfolio checklist and presentation draft.

Language Objective: Students will use process language:

First, I...
Next, I checked...

11.4 Core Idea

A portfolio is a collection of your work. It shows your learning over time.

A presentation draft is the first version of what you will say or submit. It does not need to be perfect today. It needs to be clear, safe, and ready to improve in Lesson 10.

11.5 Key Vocabulary

Word Simple Meaning Example Sentence
portfolio A collection of work My portfolio shows my learning.
checklist A list to check completion I used a checklist.
draft A first version I wrote a presentation draft.
presentation A short talk or written share I practiced my presentation.
workflow A repeatable process My workflow helps me check AI answers.
reflection Thinking about learning My reflection explains what improved.
prepare Get ready I prepare for Lesson 10.

11.8.2 Choose a Final Project Task

Choose one real but safe task:

  • choose a study plan
  • prepare for a job interview
  • compare transportation options
  • plan a weekly budget with fake numbers
  • write a polite request for help
  • summarize a public rule or announcement
  • create a simple English learning plan

Avoid high-stakes decisions where AI could cause harm. For health, legal, immigration, tax, financial contract, safety emergency, school discipline, or work contract topics, use AI only to organize questions or learn vocabulary. Ask a qualified person.
