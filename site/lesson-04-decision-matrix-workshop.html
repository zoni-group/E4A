6.1 Start Here

In Lesson 3 and the My Dream Decision homework, you wrote a prompt, a simple matrix, and a final decision reason. Today you will use that work to create or improve a draft decision matrix. You will revise your prompt, ask AI or a partner for review, and decide which suggestions to accept or reject.

The final decision stays with you.

6.2 Why This Matters

Good decision-making is not one step. You draft, check, revise, and explain. AI can suggest criteria or risks, but you must ask good questions, check assumptions, and decide what fits your real situation.

Some exercises in the website version are interactive. You can type your answers directly on the page, and you can copy or download a backup if your teacher asks.

6.3 Today You Will

By the end of this lesson, you will:

  1. Revise a prompt using the ZONI CLEAR scaffold.
  2. Create or improve an AI-assisted draft decision matrix.
  3. Ask AI, a partner, or a sample output for review without letting AI decide.
  4. Accept or reject suggestions in English.
  5. Complete the matching Student Workbook page.
NoteLesson Reference

Theme: Better decisions come from review, evidence, and human judgment.

AI Focus: CLEAR prompt revision and AI review without AI decision-making.

ESL Focus: Revision language, reasons, accept/reject language, and comparison.

Content Objective: Students will revise one CLEAR prompt and create or improve one AI-assisted decision matrix.

Language Objective: Students will use simple English:

I improved it by...
One useful suggestion was...
I will not use this because...
Before I decide, I need to check...

6.4 Core Idea

A revision is an improved version. You can revise a prompt by adding context, limits, output format, or a review instruction.

An AI answer is not automatically correct. You can accept helpful suggestions, reject weak suggestions, change scores or criteria, and write your reason in English.

6.5 Key Vocabulary

Word Simple Meaning Example Sentence
revise Improve after checking I revised my prompt.
matrix A table for comparing choices I used a decision matrix.
draft A first version to check and improve AI made a draft matrix.
suggestion An idea someone gives you The AI suggestion was useful.
accept Decide to use something I accepted one suggestion.
reject Decide not to use something I rejected a risky suggestion.
assumption Something I think is true but need to check My assumption is that the bus runs at night.
verify Check if something is true I need to verify the price.
final decision The choice I make AI helps, but I make the final decision.

6.8 Guided Practice

6.8.1 AI Review Prompt

Use this review prompt with a safe matrix:

Help me review this decision matrix.
Do not make the final decision for me.
Use simple English.
Tell me:
1. One criterion I should add.
2. One assumption I should check.
3. One risk I should think about.
4. One score or weight I should review.
5. One suggestion I should reject if it does not fit my situation.

6.8.2 Accept or Reject

Sample suggestion:

Add "energy" as a criterion.

Accept sentence:

I will use this suggestion because my energy changes during the day.

Sample suggestion:

Choose the highest-scoring option automatically.

Reject sentence:

I will not use this suggestion because AI should not make the final decision for me.

Now write your own accept and reject sentences.
